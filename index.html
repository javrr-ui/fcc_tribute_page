Kentarō Miura

Biography

Kentaro Miura (三浦 建太郎, Miura Kentarō, July 11, 1966 – May 6, 2021) was a Japanese manga artist. He was best known for his acclaimed dark fantasy series Berserk, which began serialization in 1989 and continued until his death. As of 2021, Berserk had more than 50 million copies in circulation, making it one of the best-selling manga series of all time. In 2002, Miura received the Award for Excellence at the 6th Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prizes.

Here's a time line of Kentaro's life

  • 1966 - Born in Chiba, Chiba Prefecture, Japan
  • 1976 - At the age of 10, Miura created his first manga titled Miuranger for his class mates.
  • 1979 - While still in Middle School, Miura began using rasters for his work, and trained himself to use professional drawing methods.
  • 1982 - He enrolled in an artistic curriculum in high school, where together with his friends they had their work published in school booklets.
  • 1985 - Miura was accepted onto an art course at Niho Daigaku University. He started two more works during this time: Futatabi and Noa.
  • 1988 - Came the first appearance of Berserk in the form of a 48-page manga now known as Berserk: The Prototype.
  • 1989 - The full serialization of Berserk, which would become Miura's most famous and successful work, began in Hakusensha's Monthly Animal House.
  • 1997 - Miura supervised the production of a 25-episode anime adaptation of Berserk produced by OLM, Inc., which aired in the same year on NTV.
  • 2002 - Miura received the Award for Excellence at the 6th Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prizes for Berserk.
  • 2006 - Berserk went on frequent and often extended hiatuses,[16] and alternated between monthly and irregular serialization.
  • 2013 - Miura released the short standalone manga Giganto Maxia, published in English-speaking territories by Dark Horse in 2016.
  • 2019 - Duranki, a short manga produced by Miura's personal manga studio Studio Gaga, was serialized in Young Animal Zero.
  • 2021 - On May 6, 2021, Miura died due to an acute aortic dissection, at the age of 54. His death was announced on May 20, 2021.
